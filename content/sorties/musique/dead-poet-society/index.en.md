## The Band

Dead Poet Society is dark, intense alternative rock, formed in 2013 by four students at Berklee College of Music in Boston — Jack Underkofler (vocals/guitar), Jack Collins (guitar), Will Goodroad (bass), and Dylan Brenner (bass, replacing Nick Taylor). Now based in Los Angeles, they draw from post-grunge, progressive rock, and indie to create something both visceral and hook-laden.

The name nods to the iconic film *Dead Poets Society* — and the philosophy fits: seize the moment, refuse to conform, express what burns inside. On stage, it's a cathartic experience: relentless energy, magnetic frontman, pure intensity.

**Key influences:** Royal Blood, Queens of the Stone Age, Highly Suspect, Nothing But Thieves, Led Zeppelin.

## Fission (2024)

Their second album on Spinefarm is an explosive, jubilant cocktail — turbulent, anthemic, driven by themes of struggle and personal transformation. The riffs are powerful and precise, the melodies introspective, and Jack Underkofler's voice oscillates between contained rage and soaring vulnerability.

**Essential tracks:**
- *My Condition* — the single that encapsulates their sound
- *Running In Circles* — unstoppable live
- *HURT* (feat. The Warning) — explosive collaboration with the Mexican trio
- *81 Tonnes* — heavy and addictive

## Live

Dead Poet Society are first and foremost a live band. They've toured with Badflower, Biffy Clyro, and Highly Suspect, and played major European festivals (Graspop, Reading & Leeds, 2000Trees, Welcome to Rockville). Their set is intense, physical, cathartic — the kind of show you walk out of drained but alive.

They played Le Rocher de Palmer (Bordeaux) in July 2025 and are booked by AEG Presents France — keep an eye out for future European dates.

**In 2026:** new single *Sinner Systems* (May 2026), US "Tour of Yellow Roses" with Highly Suspect, and a new album on the way. No European dates announced yet, but it's only a matter of time.
