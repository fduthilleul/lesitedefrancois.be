Speciality coffee (also spelled *specialty coffee*) is coffee scored above 80/100 by a Q-grader, with full traceability from farm to cup — origin, variety, processing method, and roast profile known and valued.

The addresses below are places I have tried on my travels, sorted by country.

- **Coffee Insurrection:** [https://www.coffeeinsurrection.com/](https://www.coffeeinsurrection.com/)
- **European Coffee Trip:** [https://europeancoffeetrip.com/](https://europeancoffeetrip.com/)
- **The World's 100 Best Coffee Shops:** [https://theworlds100bestcoffeeshops.com/](https://theworlds100bestcoffeeshops.com/)

## 🇧🇪 Belgium

{{< coffee-shop name="Alchemists Coffee" location="Rue de la Crèche 21, 1050 Ixelles" url="https://alchemists.coffee/" >}}
An open laboratory and neighbourhood roastery in Ixelles, where Xavier Beressy and his team roast specialty coffees on-site. Simple, lively atmosphere, standout filter coffees and barista workshops. Belgian Aeropress Champion 2024.
{{< /coffee-shop >}}

{{< coffee-shop name="DRACHE Specialty Coffee Bar" location="Quai au Bois à Brûler 11, 1000 Brussels" url="https://www.instagram.com/drache.bxl/" >}}
A cosy coffee shop on the fish market quay (Vismet), known for a creative menu: classic espressos, cold brew, and signature lattes (butterfly pea, beetroot, ube, black sesame…). Small pastry selection with vegan options.
{{< /coffee-shop >}}

{{< coffee-shop name="Gust Coffee Roasters" location="A. Vaucampslaan 28, 1654 Beersel" url="https://gust.coffee/" >}}
Belgian micro-roastery south of Brussels, founded by Tanguy in 2020. Seasonal, traceable and ethical coffees, roasted to highlight each origin's clarity and character. Online shop and subscriptions from the roastery.
{{< /coffee-shop >}}

{{< coffee-shop name="Jolicoeur" location="Rue d'Enghien 3, 7000 Mons" url="https://jolicoeurcoffee.be/" >}}
A warm little coffee shop near Mons' Grand-Place, run by Ludovic Pirrera — a globe-trotting barista who roasts his own beans in Ghlin. Bold espresso, V60 and Aeropress, beans for sale and cupping workshops. Traceable cooperative sourcing.
{{< /coffee-shop >}}

{{< coffee-shop name="MOK Coffee" location="Rue Antoine Dansaert 196, 1000 Brussels" url="https://mokcoffee.be/" >}}
A pioneering Belgian specialty roaster, founded in Leuven in 2012 by Jens Crabbé, two-time Belgian Cup Tasters champion. The Brussels flagship on Dansaert — housed in a former art gallery — combines roasting, an open bar, two rotating espressos and four filter options. Ranked 67th on [The World's 100 Best Coffee Shops](https://theworlds100bestcoffeeshops.com/locales/mok-coffee/).

*Also at Diestsestraat 165, 3000 Leuven · Rue Saint-Laurent 36, 1000 Brussels (MOK Studio).*
{{< /coffee-shop >}}

{{< coffee-shop name="Tulipe" location="Avenue Albert-Elisabeth 35, 1200 Woluwe-Saint-Lambert" url="https://www.tulipe.coffee/" >}}
A specialty café born from a simple idea: quality coffee that's accessible in Woluwe-Saint-Lambert. Carefully selected beans, partner roasters, full traceability and a welcoming atmosphere — small producers, espresso and filter in a friendly setting.
{{< /coffee-shop >}}

{{< coffee-shop name="Wide Awake" location="Rue Sainte-Catherine 2, 1000 Brussels" url="https://wideawake.coffee/" >}}
A contemporary Brussels roaster sourcing, roasting and serving exceptional coffees. City-centre flagship (Sainte-Catherine), plus Flagey (Lesbroussart) and Dansaert (rue de Flandre). « Released on 12 » series, subscriptions and wholesale.
{{< /coffee-shop >}}

## 🇫🇷 France

{{< coffee-shop name="Coffee Makers" location="151 Rue de Paris, 59800 Lille" url="https://coffeemakers.fr/" >}}
A Lille institution since 2013: espresso bar, tea room and certified organic artisan roaster (Ludovic Fiers). Breakfast, Saturday brunch, homemade pastries, matcha and hot chocolate in a cosy city-centre coffee shop.
{{< /coffee-shop >}}

## 🇨🇦 Canada

{{< coffee-shop name="Café Différance" location="449 Avenue Viger Ouest, Montréal" url="https://www.instagram.com/cafedifferance/" >}}
Quality coffee stop in Old Montreal, steps from Square-Victoria. Rotating Canadian roasters (including Bows & Arrows), friendly vibe and pastries from Hof Kelsten and Godley & Creme. The name is a philosophical wink — with an « A », not an « E ».
{{< /coffee-shop >}}

{{< coffee-shop name="Little Victories" location="44 Elgin Street, Ottawa" url="https://www.lvcoffee.ca/pages/visit" >}}
A small independent Ottawa roaster, co-founded by a roaster and a barista, with three cafés and a wholesale operation. Careful coffee culture, warm hospitality and a commitment to raising the Canadian scene. Ranked 71st on [The World's 100 Best Coffee Shops](https://theworlds100bestcoffeeshops.com/locales/little-victories-coffee/).
{{< /coffee-shop >}}

## 🇰🇷 South Korea

{{< coffee-shop name="tonti" location="6-8 Bukchon-ro, Jongno-gu, Seoul" url="https://www.instagram.com/tonti_coffee/" >}}
Steps from Anguk station and Changdeokgung Palace, tonti hides in the quiet alleys at the foot of Bukchon Hanok Village. Creamy filter coffee, Dubancho and Ethiopian Banko Gotti, soft cookies — an affordable, photogenic stop between hanok houses and sloping lanes.
{{< /coffee-shop >}}

{{< coffee-shop name="Cha.ddeul" location="Seoul (Bukchon)" url="https://www.instagram.com/cha.ddeul" >}}
A favourite for atmosphere and views — but to be clear: it's more of a traditional tea house than a specialty coffee shop. Cha.ddeul is one of Bukchon's most peaceful addresses, set in a hanok at a turn in the village lanes. Silent inner courtyard, Korean teas, delicate desserts and filter coffee in a preserved, almost meditative setting.

Between Changdeokgung Palace and a stroll through Bukchon Hanok Village, the perfect place to slow down after Seoul's bustle — especially in autumn when leaves fall in the garden.
{{< /coffee-shop >}}
